# STAY SAFE ONLINE

•	Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always activate 2FA on your college email, LinkedIn, and social media accounts to add a vital layer of security beyond just a password.

•	Verify UPI 'Request Money' Phishing: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money; if someone asks you to enter a PIN to get a payment, it is a scam.

•	Secure Your QR Code Scans: Before scanning a QR code for a UPI payment at a local vendor or online, double-check that the displayed name matches the intended recipient.

•	Audit Your Digital Footprint: Regularly search for your name online and adjust privacy settings on platforms like Instagram to ensure only trusted individuals can see your personal posts.

•	Practice Email Hygiene: Never click on "urgent" links in emails claiming you have won a prize or that your college account is "blocked" without verifying the sender's official address.

•	Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Avoid using easily guessable information like your name or birthdate; use a combination of symbols, numbers, and letters for every unique platform.

•	Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Do not log into your bank accounts or enter credit card details while using free, unsecured public Wi-Fi networks.

•	Check Before You Share: Before forwarding news or "confessions" about peers on WhatsApp groups, verify the source to prevent the spread of misinformation and potential cyberbullying.

# How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know is a victim of an online threat, use the following official channels:

•	National Cyber Crime Portal: (cybercrime.gov.in)

•	24/7 Helpline Number: Dial 1930 immediately for financial frauds or harassment
